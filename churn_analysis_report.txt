💡 KEY INSIGHTS & RECOMMENDATIONS
====================================================================================================

🔍 PRIMARY CHURN PATTERN:
   → Churned customers exhibit SIGNIFICANTLY LOWER engagement across ALL metrics
   → This suggests dissatisfaction with content or poor user experience

📋 STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS:

   1. EARLY WARNING SYSTEM
      • Monitor customers with <70 viewing hours/month
      • Flag users with declining session duration trends
      • Alert when binge-watching sessions drop below 6/month

   2. PROACTIVE ENGAGEMENT
      • Send personalized content recommendations to low-engagement users
      • Implement re-engagement campaigns for users watching <20 unique titles
      • Create curated playlists to encourage content discovery

   3. CUSTOMER SERVICE IMPROVEMENT
      • Investigate why churned customers needed MORE support
      • Address common issues proactively before they escalate
      • Implement feedback loops after CS interactions

   4. TIER-SPECIFIC STRATEGIES
      • Basic tier: Highest churn (43.5%) - consider better value proposition
      • Premium tier: Lowest churn (24.1%) - identify what works and replicate
      • Offer promotional upgrades to Standard/Premium for at-risk Basic users

   5. CONTENT STRATEGY
      • Horror & Thriller genres show highest churn - expand content library
      • Documentary & Comedy viewers are more loyal - promote these genres
      • Improve recommendation algorithms for high-churn genres

====================================================================================================
✅ CONCLUSION
====================================================================================================

   The analysis reveals that churn is primarily driven by LOW ENGAGEMENT rather than
   dissatisfaction with specific features. Customers who churn watch less content, have
   shorter sessions, and explore fewer titles. This indicates either:
   • Content not matching their preferences
   • Poor discovery/recommendation experience
   • Competition from other platforms

   The high correlation between CS interactions and churn suggests technical issues
   or service problems may be pushing customers away. Addressing these root causes
   through improved recommendations, better content, and proactive support can
   significantly reduce churn rates.
